Yangtze River

The Yangtze river is one of the two major rivers in ancient China. In the early days of ancient China, it allowed the people to make civilizations in river valleys. In later china, it provided fresh water and transportation.




Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is one of the most well known architectural accomplishments of the ancient world. At first, the Great Wall was built in multiple seperate section in order to protect individual dynasties. Eventually, once China was united, all of these walls were joined together, forming the Great Wall of China.




Imperial Palace

The Imperial Palace was constructed 1406-1420 CE by the Ming emperor Zhu Di. After the construction its construction, 24 emperors, their families, and servants all lived in the palace at the same time.




Shaolin Temple

The Shaolin Temple is recognized as the birthplace of Chan Buddhism and Shaolin Kung Fu. Legend says that Bodhidharma arrived at the Shaolin Temple at around 527 BCE.




Himalayn Mountains

The Himalayn mountains are to the South and Sotheast of China. They are the largest mountains in the world, giving the Chinese natural protection in their earliest years.




Gobi Desert

The Gobi Desert is a cold desert in the Northern part of China. In the early world, the Mongols would live in this desert and continuously invade China. This is one of the reasons that the Great Wall of China was constructed.




Silk Road

The silk road was an extensive trade route that connected China to the West. This trade route was called the silk road because of how much silk China would export using it.
