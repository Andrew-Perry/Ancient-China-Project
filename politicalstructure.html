Xia Dynasty

The picture shown on the left displays Yu The Great, the founder of the Xia Dynasty. The Xia Dynasty was the first government system to emerge in Ancient China. They set the standard for how the government should work in China’s future. They set in place a dynasty, meaning that leadership is hereditary, and this particular dynasty was founded in 1600 BCE and was taken over in 1046 BCE. Yu was able to establish his own form of government with him at the top because he helped save the people from a serious flood, earning him the trust of the people and boosting his reputation. Of course, Yu eventually died and passed the rule onto his son Qi which began the tradition of passing leadership down through the bloodline.




Warring States Period

The Warring States Period was a major political conflict that took place from 481-221 BCE. The main reason for this conflict was because many lands broke off of the central government and declared their own independence. During this time, seven different states fought each other for control over China. During the war, a skilled pacifist engineer named Mo Ti shared the war technologies among all seven states hoping that this would prevent any of them from getting the upper hand and thus ending the warring states period. However this didn't work, as Qin ended up defeating Zhao. An influential general in Qin’s army named Sun-Tzu wrote the famous book “The Art of War” during this time period and many claim it was his military reforms that ensured success over the other states.




Tang Dynasty

The Tang Dynasty is seen as the “Golden Age” of Ancient China, taking place from 618-907 CE. It is looked back on as an age of reform and cultural advancements which can still be seen in China today. The picture shown on the left depicts a man named emperor Xuanzong who led China to become the most successful country in the world at the time. Significant advancements in science, medicine, technology, literature and architecture were brought about during this era. One major reason for the prosperity of this time was the reforms that were made to the law and nation as a whole. These reforms were so popular that they were even copied by other nations such as Japan and Korea.






Qin Shi Huang

Qin Shi Huang, as shown on the right, is a former emperor of Ancient China. His biggest accomplishment was unifying Ancient China, and he additionally started the foundation for the Great Wall of China. He was an emperor in the Qin dynasty which had just come out on top in the warring states period. With its power, Qin developed a bureaucratic government, along with a totalitarian state philosophy called legalism. Totalitarianism is a form of government in which the people at the top have total power and control over the nation. Qin Shi Huang was also successful in standardizing a writing system which was part of the unification of China. Qin Shi Huang is also famous for his construction of the Terracotta Army which he had built to protect him in the afterlife by stationed outside of his tomb.
