Silk Road

The silk road was a major source of income for Ancient China. It was a network of trade routes that was officially created during the Han Dynasty. The reason the trade network was called the silk road was because silk was one of the major exports from China that was in high demand from other nations at the time. A few other exports were tea, dyes, precious stones, china, spices, rice, paper, and gunpowder. All of which were highly sought after by other countries and resulted in China becoming richer than most of the world. The network was widely used until the Ottoman Empire boycotted trade and ended up closing the routes.




The Yuanbao/Currency

The Yuanbo was the main form of currency in Ancient China. It was a circular coin that was made from copper, iron, lead, gold, and silver with different shapes and marks. Eventually the Yuanbo was left behind as in the year 960 CE China switched to a paper currency which was the first in the world to do so. An uncommon belief for the Ancient Chinese monetary system was that it was merely a means of exchange and a measure of value alone. The Chinese didn’t believe that money was a way to store value, which is why it remained low in value for a long time.




Tang Dynasty

The Tang Dynasty is seen as the “Golden Age” of Ancient China taking place from 618-907 CE. It is looked back on as an age of reform and cultural advancements which can still be seen in China today. The picture shown on the left depicts a man named emperor Xuanzong who led China to become the most successful country in the world at the time. Significant advancements in science, medicine, technolog, literature and architecture were brought about during this era. One major reason for the prosperity of this time was the reforms that were made for the law and nation as a whole. These reforms were so popular that they were even copied by other nations such as Japan and Korea.






Qin Shi Huang

Qin Shi Huang, as shown on the right, is a former emperor of Ancient China. His biggest accomplishment was unifying Ancient China, and he additionally started the foundation for the Great Wall of China. He was an emperor in the Qin dynasty which had just come out on top in the warring states period. With its power, Qin developed a bureaucratic government, along with a totalitarian state philosophy called legalism. Totalitarianism is a form of government in which the people at the top have total power and control over the nation. Qin Shi Huang was also successful in standardizing a writing system which was part of the unification of China. Qin Shi Huang is also famous for his construction of the Terracotta Army which he had built to protect him in the afterlife by stationed outside of his tomb.
