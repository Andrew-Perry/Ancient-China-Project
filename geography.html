Rivers

There were two major rivers in ancient China: the yellow river which is in the Northern part of China and the Yangtze river which is in the southern part of China. These rivers provided fertile soil for growing crops, fresh water, and transportation. These rivers also had ideological benefits as a lot of their literature and art was based around these rivers. The yellow river was also where the civilization began, being used as a river valley.




Mountains

To the South and Southeast of China are the Himalayan mountains. These mountains are the highest mountains in the wordld, thus providing a nearly impossible to cross border that gave isolation and natural protection from possible invaders. This isolation allowed the Chinese to develop and grow their civilization in peace. The mountains were also an important part of Chinese religion and were thus sacred to the people.
